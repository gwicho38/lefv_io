Week 7: Workflows

Overview

In this week and the two weeks that follow, we will look at data cleaning and analysis in the context of workflows and provenance. Workflows are used to describe and automate data cleaning and analysis "pipelines." Workflows can also combine manual or semi-automatic steps (e.g., in OpenRefine) with fully automatic steps (e.g., using Python, R scripts, or SQL queries). We start by studying scientific workflows (i.e., where scientists and researchers automate their data processing and analysis in dedicated systems or using general purposes scripting languages).

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, you will be able to:

  • Understand the role of workflows and provenance in the data life-cycle.

  • Explain how workflows (as prospective provenance) are related to (traditional retrospective) provenance.

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Workflow automation

  • Workflows as prospective provenance

  • Computational reproducibility

  • Provenance standards (OPM, W3C PROV)

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the module.

  • What are (scientific) workflows used for? What advantages do they bring to researchers?

  • Why do many researchers prefer to use scripting technologies (such as R and Python) over dedicated workflow technologies?

  • How are workflows and provenance related?

  • What is the importance of workflows and provenance in the context of the larger data life-cycle and data cleaning?
