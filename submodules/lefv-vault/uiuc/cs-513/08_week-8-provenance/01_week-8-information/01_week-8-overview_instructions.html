Week 8: Provenance

Overview

In this week we continue with workflows and provenance, focusing on the latter. We take both a wider and a closer look at provenance, including standards such as OPM (Open Provenance Model) and W3C PROV (the official standard of the World Wide Web consortium). We also relate provenance to current uses (e.g., in the National Climate Assessment or a large cyberinfrastructure project [DataONE]).

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, you will be able to:

  • Understand the similarities and differences between different notions of provenance.

  • Understand the role of provenance in data cleaning and the larger data life-cycle.

  • Identify what kinds of approaches can be used for modeling and querying provenance.

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Workflows and prospective provenance ("workflow land")

  • Runtime observables and retrospective provenance ("trace land")

  • Hybrid forms of provenance

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the module.

  • What are similarities and differences between workflows and provenance models?

  • How can workflows and provenance information be used individually and in combination?

  • What questions and queries can be answered using a workflow model and/or provenance information?
