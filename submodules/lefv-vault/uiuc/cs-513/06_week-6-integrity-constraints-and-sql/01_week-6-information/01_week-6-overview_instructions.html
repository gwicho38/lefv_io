Week 6: Integrity Constraints and SQL

Overview

In this week we move from the theoretical and logical foundations of relational data to its main practical incarnation: SQL, the structured query language. We will learn how SQL can be used to formulate queries, which in turn can be used to check the integrity of data similar to the way in which Datalog queries and rules in denial form were used before.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, you will be able to:

  • Transfer ideas from Datalog to SQL (i.e., expressing ...

  • ... relational queries and

  • ... relational integrity constraints in SQL).

  • Use SQLite to load and query datasets (that might have been preprocessed and partially cleaned with OpenRefine).

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Different types of SQL database systems (traditional multi-user, client-server systems vs. SQLite)

  • SELECT-FROM-WHERE queries in SQL

  • Queries as a means to profile and check data

  • Recursive queries

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the module.

  • How can relational queries and integrity constraints be used to explore profile data and to check the integrity of data?

  • How can you write queries in SQL that filter, combine, or aggregate data?

  • Why and when should you use SQL compared to OpenRefine or Datalog, for example?
