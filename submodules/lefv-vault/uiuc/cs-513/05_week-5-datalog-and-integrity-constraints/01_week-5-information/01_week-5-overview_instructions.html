Week 5: Datalog and Integrity Constraints

Overview

Simple validation and transformation techniques can be used to address syntactic errors in data, but are not able to address logical errors. For example, they can be used to validate and normalize ZIP code and state abbreviations, but not determine whether the ZIP code/state combination is valid. Logic errors can be identified through the definition of data quality rules in the form of integrity constraints such as functional and inclusion dependencies. Integrity constraints are expressed as logic rules or queries.

In this module we introduce Datalog, a rule-based language for expressing relational queries and integrity constraints. Integrity constraints can be defined and checked with Datalog thus complementing the pattern-based data cleaning in OpenRefine with logic-based data quality checks. We begin with Datalog, because it makes clear the underlying logical kernel of queries and integrity constraints that is sometimes obscured in languages such as SQL.

Goals and Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, you will be able to:

  • Identify integrity constraints such as key constraints, functional dependencies, denial constraints

  • Formulate and execute queries and check integrity constraints as rules in Datalog.

  • Recognize and deal with common Datalog pitfalls (e.g., rules that are not "safe").

Key Phrases/Concepts

Keep your eyes open for the following key terms or phrases as you interact with the lectures and complete the activities.

  • Datalog rules and queries

  • Expressing and checking integrity constraints (ICs) in Datalog

  • "Denial form" of ICs in Datalog

  • Database repair as constraint solving

Guiding Questions

Develop your answers to the following guiding questions while watching lectures and working on assignments throughout the module.

  • What are queries and integrity constraints and how can they be used for data cleaning?

  • What data quality issues can be tackled by relational queries and integrity constraints (versus issues we could or couldn't tackle in OpenRefine)?

  • What is the difference between checking integrity constraints and repairing constraints?
