Using the LTI Launchers to Connect to PrairieLearn

The "LTI" items in this course launch our external assignment tool, PrairieLearn. This is an external website hosted by the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

It's important that you navigate to individual PrairieLearn assignments using the LTI launcher pages on this Coursera site . For example, the Recursion Quiz item on Coursera will help you sign your name and launch the first PrairieLearn assignment:

Pictured above: When you click the "Open Tool" button (here highlighted in orange), it will launch PrairieLearn with your own unique login credentials, and take you directly to the correct page. This is the correct way for you to log in to PrairieLearn and go to an assignment on PrairieLearn throughout the course.

Please note , you should not try to log into the PrairieLearn website directly; that is meant for other purposes at the University. We've set up these LTI launchers on Coursera so you don't have to do that.

Please also note , you shouldn't use the "Assessments" menu on PrairieLearn to jump between different PrairieLearn assignments you are working on. (An example is shown below, highlighted in red. Please don't click that!) Instead, please always use the LTI launchers here on Coursera to go directly to the item you need.

Pictured above: You should not click the "Assessments" menu highlighted in red!

If you accidentally log into or navigate around the PrairieLearn site in the incorrect way, your assignment grades earned on PrairieLearn might not be automatically transferred back to Coursera. Don't worry too much: Then you'll just need to contact a TA to make sure your grade gets fixed. Please use the LTI launcher items on Coursera to make sure that everything stays connected smoothly.

Sorry for the added confusion! We're working on an upgrade to PrairieLearn that will make this more intuitive in the future.
